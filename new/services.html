Legalizing your stay

From your first card to an EU passport

Six services for anyone securing the right to live in Poland: temporary and permanent residence, Blue Card, citizenship — and protection if you've received a refusal.

Flagship Legalization

Residence card (karta pobytu) — temporary residence permit

Karta pobytu confirms your right to live in Poland. It's issued by the voivodeship office for 1–3 years, then renewed. We guide you from the first consultation until the card is in your hands. Grounds: work, your own business, studies, family, Polish roots, temporary protection.

  • Free legal assessment of your chances
  • Complete document package and an AI check before submission
  • Appointment at the voivodeship office — no queues
  • A specialist accompanies you to the urząd
  • Appeal after a refusal — free

Permanent residence — pobyt stały

Zezwolenie na pobyt stały is permanent residence status in Poland — the next step after a temporary card. Issued indefinitely: work without permits, free movement across the EU, and the foundation for citizenship.

  • Standard route — 5 years of continuous residence on a temporary card
  • Karta Polaka — no residence period required
  • Eligibility assessment and complete document collection
  • Translation, notarization, filing and case monitoring

Blue Card — the card for professionals

Niebieska Karta UE is a residence card for highly qualified professionals: the right to live and work in the EU. The card is issued for 2–4 years, with permanent residence possible after just 2 years and EU mobility after 18 months.

  • A university degree (at least 3 years of study) — or 5+ years of work experience
  • An employment contract for at least 1 year
  • There's a salary requirement — we'll check whether you qualify

Polish citizenship — an EU passport

Obywatelstwo polskie is a Polish passport with the full rights of an EU citizen. We handle your case from analyzing the grounds to the passport itself. Four paths — we'll determine which fits you at a consultation:

  • Naturalization — 10 years of residence, the last 3 on permanent residence, B1+ Polish
  • Karta Polaka — a fast-track procedure for people of Polish descent
  • Marriage — 3 years married + 2 years living in Poland on a residence permit
  • Repatriation — for descendants of Poles from former USSR countries

Protection after a refusal

Appeals — a refusal can be challenged

A refusal on your residence card, permanent residence or another document is not the end. We specialize in appeals and overturn refusals even when other lawyers said "no chance". Three instances:

  • Odwołanie — a standard appeal, 14 days to file
  • Skarga do WSA — a complaint to the administrative court, 30 days
  • Skarga kasacyjna — cassation to the NSA, the highest instance
  • Analysis of why you were refused and an honest opinion on your chances
Important: to file an appeal — 14 days from the date you received the refusal. If the refusal is already in your hands — message us today.

Documents

PESEL — your number in Poland

An 11-digit identification number, similar to a tax ID: without it, almost no door opens — bank account, official employment, NFZ insurance, government services, rental agreement, business registration. The state issues the number itself free of charge, on the same day.

  • Preparing your documents and accompanying you to the urząd
  • Meldunek registration — if needed
  • Getting your PESEL confirmation
  • Next step — Profil Zaufany: online access to government services

Prices and timelines in brief

What people ask most often before we start. We answer honestly.

How much do the services cost, and when will I know the price?+
The price is fixed in an official contract before work begins — no extra charges along the way. We'll name the exact amount at a free consultation. Payment — 2–3 installments to the company account.
Can I pay in installments?+
Yes. Payment is 2–3 installments to the company account. We start working without waiting for the full amount.
What happens if I get a refusal?+
If we handled your case from the start, we file the appeal free of charge. You have 14 days from the date you receive the refusal, so message us right away. We also take on refusals from elsewhere: we'll analyze the reasons and tell you honestly whether there's a chance.
